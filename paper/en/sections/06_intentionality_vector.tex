\section{Intentionality Vector (VI)}
\label{sec:vi}

The Intentionality Vector (VI) monitors manifold health and applies
corrections when it detects degradation. It functions as an ``immune
system'' for the manifold, preventing dimensional collapse and
confidence inflation.

\subsection{$\phi$ Field: Manifold Health}

Manifold health is quantified by the scalar field $\phi(\calM)$,
composed of 4 indicators:

\begin{equation}
    \phi(\calM) = \sum_{i=1}^{4} w_i \cdot \phi_i
    \label{eq:phi_field}
\end{equation}

with $\sum w_i = 1$. The 4 components are:

\paragraph{$\phi_1$: Effective Dimensionality.}
Measures how many of the 5 dimensions are active:

\begin{equation}
    \phi_1 = \frac{1}{5} \sum_{k=0}^{4} \mathbb{1}\big[\text{std}(c_k) > \epsilon_{\text{dim}}\big]
\end{equation}

Low values indicate \textbf{dimensional collapse} --- the model projects
tokens into a lower-dimensional subspace, losing epistemic capacity.

\paragraph{$\phi_2$: Dispersion.}
Measures position diversity on the manifold:

\begin{equation}
    \phi_2 = \frac{1}{5} \sum_{k=0}^{4} \text{std}(c_k)
\end{equation}

\paragraph{$\phi_3$: Directional Field Entropy.}
High entropy indicates diversified exploration; low entropy indicates
stagnation:

\begin{equation}
    \phi_3 = \frac{H(\bm{F})}{\log K}
\end{equation}

where $K$ is the number of discretization bins.

\paragraph{$\phi_4$: Confidence Distribution.}
Uses a U-shaped profile with ideal standard deviation $\sigma^* = 0.15$:

\begin{equation}
    \phi_4 = 1 - \left|\text{std}(\kappa) - \sigma^*\right| / \sigma^*
\end{equation}

This penalizes both uniformly high confidence (excess) and uniformly
low confidence (collapse).

\subsection{Severity and Activation}

Intervention severity follows a square root curve for aggressive
response in critical cases:

\begin{equation}
    s = \begin{cases}
        0 & \text{if } \phi \geq \phi_{\text{critical}} \\
        \sqrt{\frac{\phi_{\text{critical}} - \phi}{\phi_{\text{critical}}}} & \text{otherwise}
    \end{cases}
    \label{eq:vi_severity}
\end{equation}

with $\phi_{\text{critical}} = 0.5$ by default.

\subsection{Manifold Correction}

When $s > 0$, the VI injects a deterministic correction into the
manifold coordinates:

\begin{equation}
    \bm{v} = s \cdot \alpha \cdot (\bm{c}_{\text{ideal}} - \bm{c})
    \label{eq:vi_correction}
\end{equation}

where $\alpha = 0.6$ is the injection strength and
$\bm{c}_{\text{ideal}}$ is determined by the direction of greatest
degradation.

\subsection{Confidence Penalty}

When confidence is inflated (high confidence with degraded manifold),
the VI applies a penalty:

\begin{equation}
    \kappa_{\text{corrected}} = \kappa \cdot (1 - \beta \cdot s)
    \label{eq:vi_conf_penalty}
\end{equation}

with $\beta = 0.4$. This prevents the model from reporting high
confidence when the manifold is in a state of collapse.

\subsection{VI Regularization}

The VI regularization loss encourages minimum health:

\begin{equation}
    \calL_{\text{VI}} = \E\big[\text{ReLU}(\phi_{\min} - \phi)^2\big]
\end{equation}

with $\phi_{\min} = 0.3$. This ensures the manifold does not fully
degenerate during training.

\subsection{Empirical Result: Collapse Prevention}

In simulations with the ATIC system (deterministic predecessor), the VI
demonstrated:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Effective dimensionality recovery: $D_{\text{eff}}$
    from 2.2 to 3.2 (out of 5 possible)
    \item Inflated confidence reduction: from 0.60 to 0.57
    \item 3 out of 4 health indicators improved
\end{itemize}

In \aletheion{}, these mechanisms are trained end-to-end, potentially
surpassing the deterministic system's results.
